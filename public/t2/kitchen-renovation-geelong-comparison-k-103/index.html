Kitchen Renovation Geelong Comparison Explained

When evaluating a kitchen renovation in Geelong, the primary distinction lies between a cosmetic refresh and a full custom fitout. A cosmetic refresh focuses on updating the visible elements while keeping the existing layout intact. This approach typically involves replacing cabinetry, installing new benchtops, updating the splashback and swapping out older appliances. Because structural changes are avoided, the timeline is shorter and the cost is generally lower, with starting points around $8,500 depending on the finishes selected.

In contrast, a luxury or custom fitout involves a significant alteration of the space, often requiring changes to the floor plan. This level of work might include removing walls to create an open-plan living area, installing a large island bench, or integrating appliances flush with cabinetry. These projects demand premium materials such as engineered or natural stone benchtops and custom joinery with soft-close mechanisms. Consequently, the starting price for these comprehensive renovations typically sits above $45,000. Understanding this fundamental difference is the first step in accurately comparing quotes and expectations.

Material and Finish Impacts

The specific materials chosen for the renovation drive the budget significantly. For cabinetry, flat-pack options offer a cost-effective solution suitable for budget-conscious projects. However, custom cabinetry, which provides tailored storage solutions like tall pantry pull-outs and integrated appliance housings, adds to the cost and lead time. Similarly, benchtop selection creates a major price divergence. Laminate or timber surfaces are common in refresh projects, whereas engineered or natural stone is standard for premium fitouts due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

Splashbacks and appliance choices further differentiate the tiers. Tile splashbacks are generally more affordable, while glass or stone splashbacks contribute to a higher-end finish. Appliances also play a role; freestanding units are typical for standard renovations, but integrated appliances that blend seamlessly with cabinetry are a hallmark of custom designs. These choices do not just affect the upfront cost but also the longevity and functionality of the kitchen.

Timeline Variations

Timeframes vary considerably based on the scope of work. While a standard renovation takes four to eight weeks on site, the lead time before construction begins is equally critical. Design, material selection and trade scheduling usually consume two to three months. Custom cabinetry specifically requires around six weeks to manufacture once the design is finalised. This lead time is sequential, meaning the cabinetry build cannot start until the design is locked in, and installation cannot proceed until the cabinets are delivered.

Furthermore, stone benchtops require a separate template-to-install window of one to three weeks after the cabinetry is fitted. Homeowners must account for these cumulative durations. Projects involving structural changes or unexpected upgrades to older wiring and plumbing, particularly in pre-1960s homes common in central Geelong, may face delays. Comparing timelines effectively requires asking specialists not just for the installation duration, but for the total time from the initial design meeting to the final handover.

Vetting Your Specialist

The regulatory environment in Victoria provides clear benchmarks for vetting tradespeople. Under the Victorian Building Act 1993, any domestic building work valued over $10,000 must be performed by a registered building practitioner. This registration is managed through the Victorian Building Authority or the Building and Plumbing Commission. For a full kitchen fitout, which almost invariably exceeds this value, checking this registration is non-negotiable.

Beyond mandatory builder registration, specific trades require their own credentials. Plumbers must be registered with the VBA or BPC, while electricians need licensing from Energy Safe Victoria. While industry memberships with bodies like the Housing Industry Association or Master Builders Australia are voluntary, they often indicate a commitment to professional standards. A robust comparison process involves verifying these credentials and ensuring the written contract explicitly states whether electrical and plumbing upgrades are included in the quote, especially in older homes where such upgrades are frequently necessary.

  1. Define the Scope. Determine if the goal is a cosmetic refresh of the existing layout or a full structural change to the room.
  2. Set a Material Budget. Choose between cost-effective options like laminate and flat-packs or premium selections like stone and custom joinery.
  3. Check Registrations. Verify that the builder is registered with the VBA for jobs over $10,000 and that plumbers and electricians are appropriately licensed.
  4. Review the Contract. Ensure the quote details all inclusions, specifically checking if electrical and plumbing upgrades are separate line items.

Common questions

What is the main cost difference between a refresh and a custom renovation? The primary difference comes from labour and materials. A refresh updates surfaces within the existing layout, starting around $8,500. A custom renovation involves structural changes, premium stone and bespoke joinery, typically starting from $45,000.

How long does the entire process take from design to finish? The total process usually includes two to three months for design and planning. This is followed by four to eight weeks of on-site construction, plus additional time for custom cabinetry manufacturing and stone templating.

Why do some quotes exclude plumbing and electrical work? In older homes, particularly pre-1960s weatherboards, wiring and plumbing often need upgrading to meet modern standards. Specialists may quote this separately to avoid surprising the homeowner with variable costs hidden in a lump sum.
