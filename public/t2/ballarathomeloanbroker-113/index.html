Ballarat Home Loan Broker Costs Explained

The primary concern for many borrowers is whether using a ballarat home loan broker adds to the expense of buying a home. For standard residential home loans, the service is typically free for the borrower. Brokers are paid a commission by the lender once the loan settles, which means you do not pay a consulting fee or an hourly rate for their time or research. This model applies whether you are buying your first home, refinancing, or investing, provided the loan settles through a lender on their panel.

While there is no direct charge, brokers operate under a best interests duty. Since January 2021, legislation requires them to act in your best interests rather than recommending a loan simply because it pays a higher commission. This regulation ensures that the cost structure of the broker does not negatively impact the recommendations you receive. The focus remains on finding a product that suits your financial situation rather than maximising the broker's payout.

Lenders Mortgage Insurance and Deposits

While the broker's service is free, the cost of the loan itself varies significantly based on your deposit size. A deposit under 20 percent of the property value typically triggers Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). This insurance protects the lender, not the borrower, but the borrower pays the premium, which can amount to thousands of dollars. This is often the most significant upfront cost for first home buyers who do not have a full 20 percent deposit saved.

Government schemes can alter these costs. The First Home Guarantee allows eligible buyers to purchase with a 5 percent deposit without paying LMI. From October 2025, this scheme is available to all eligible buyers, removing the LMI cost barrier for those with smaller savings. A broker can identify which lenders participate in these schemes and how the waiver affects your total loan amount and repayments. Checking scheme eligibility early is essential to avoid paying unnecessary insurance premiums.

Refinancing and Rate Review Expenses

For existing homeowners, the cost of staying on an uncompetitive loan can be far higher than switching. A rate review through a broker is a free service that compares your current rate against the market. If your rate is no longer competitive, refinancing can lower your monthly repayments and reduce the total interest paid over the life of the loan. While some lenders charge discharge or settlement fees when you leave, the long-term savings from a lower rate often outweigh these one-off costs.

Refinancing also allows you to access equity for renovations or debt consolidation. This process involves a new valuation and a reassessment of your loan-to-value ratio. There are no costs associated with the broker conducting this investigation. You only proceed if the numbers make sense, meaning the potential savings or benefits of accessing equity justify the switching costs involved.

Investment and Construction Loan Structures

Investors and those building new homes face different cost considerations. For investors, the structure of the loan affects tax deductibility and cash flow. Interest-only periods can minimise initial repayments, which may suit certain investment strategies, although the total interest paid over the loan term may be higher. A mortgage broker in Ballarat can compare these structures to ensure they align with your investment goals without incurring unnecessary interest charges.

Construction loans require specific funding facilities where money is released in stages as building milestones are reached. Interest is generally charged only on the amount drawn down, not the total loan limit. While the setup for construction finance can be more complex, using a broker to find a lender experienced with progress draws ensures you are not paying interest on funds you cannot yet use. This specialised matching helps manage cash flow during the build process.

Common questions

Does using a broker cost more than going to a bank? No, the service is typically free for the borrower as the broker is paid by the lender. Furthermore, brokers compare rates from multiple lenders, which can result in a lower interest rate than walking into a single bank branch.

Are there fees for refinancing through a broker? The broker's service to arrange refinancing is free. However, your existing lender may charge discharge fees and your new lender may charge establishment fees, which are standard costs of switching loans.

How does the First Home Guarantee affect costs? It allows eligible buyers to purchase with a 5 percent deposit without paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance, saving thousands in upfront insurance costs.
