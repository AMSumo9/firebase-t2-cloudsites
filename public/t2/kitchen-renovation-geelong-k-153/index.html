Kitchen Renovation Geelong Explained

Understanding what actually moves the price helps you allocate your budget effectively. A small or budget refresh keeps the existing footprint and updates cabinetry, benchtop, splashback and appliances, starting from around $8,500. In contrast, a premium or custom fitout typically begins from $45,000+, driven significantly by your choice of materials and finishes.

Cabinetry is a primary cost factor. Flat pack options keep a budget refresh affordable, while custom cabinetry featuring soft close drawers, tall pantry pull-outs and integrated appliance housings sits at the higher end. Benchtop choices similarly split the budget; laminate or timber suits a refresh, whereas engineered or natural stone pushes the project toward the premium band. Splashbacks and appliances follow the same pattern, with tile and freestanding units costing less than glass or stone surfaces and integrated appliances. A firm figure is only possible once a specialist has seen the space and confirmed these specific choices, as finish level often impacts the price more than the floor area itself.

Timeline expectations and scheduling

Once work begins on site, a standard kitchen renovation in Geelong typically takes 4-8 weeks. This on-site period follows 2-3 months of design and lead-time planning beforehand, which covers layout sign-off, material selection and trade scheduling. It is important to note that certain lead times run in sequence rather than parallel, which is why planning needs to start well before demolition day.

Custom cabinetry has its own specific build time, usually around 6 weeks from final design approval. Additionally, stone benchtops require a separate template-to-install window of 1-3 weeks once the cabinetry is in place and can be accurately measured. These sequential steps, design, cabinetry build, then templating and installation, mean that the total project duration is cumulative. Anyone weighing a full rebuild against joinery alone should also consider cabinetry specialists, as the cabinetry lead time is often what sets the entire project schedule.

Registration and licensing checks

Before signing any contract, verifying the credentials of your tradespeople is a non-negotiable step. Under the Victorian Building Act 1993, domestic building work over $10,000 must be carried out by a registered building practitioner. Since most full renovations cross this threshold once cabinetry, benchtops and installation costs are combined, confirming registration is a basic first check for homeowners in Newtown, Belmont and Highton.

Specific checks include ensuring builders are registered with the Victorian Building Authority or Building and Plumbing Commission for domestic building work over $10,000. Plumbers must be registered with the VBA/BPC, and electricians must be licensed with Energy Safe Victoria. While industry memberships with the Housing Industry Association or Master Builders Victoria are not mandatory, they serve as a further signal of an established operator. However, none of these checks replace the importance of reading and understanding the written contract.

Housing stock and project variables

The age and type of your home significantly influence the scope and potential cost of a renovation. Newtown and central Geelong have a high volume of pre-1960s housing stock, where original wiring and plumbing often cannot handle a modern layout without an upgrade. This necessary work is frequently quoted separately from the renovation itself.

In contrast, Belmont and Highton feature more 1970s to 90s brick veneer homes, which generally offer easier access for cabinetry delivery and fewer structural surprises. Ocean Grove presents a mix of older beach shacks and newer builds on larger blocks. Because of these differences, a written quote should clearly state whether electrical or plumbing upgrades are included or separate. This single line item often explains most price discrepancies between quotes for what appears to be the same job on paper.
